Our girls are moving up

And with your help, so will we.

We were founded in 2009, and by 2010 we organized our first tie-dye cupcake bake sale, raising money to start empowering girls. By October 2011 we grew as a company and hosted our first Leadership Summit on the UN’s International Day of the Girl. We graduated our first girl in June 2012 and are now expanding our operations into two new countries.

A typical girl going through the Shanti Bhavan program in India comes from a life of poverty. She is admitted at four and is educated through age seventeen. At the end of her education, a girl that has graduated from Shanti Bhavan has the chance to earn a wage 18% higher than a girl that has not received a degree.

Why your help matters

Introducing Speakers

Tammy Tibbets

President and Founder of She's The First

Tammy is the first in her family to graduate from college–she studied journalism at The College of New Jersey, where her final assignment unexpectedly put her on the path to social entrepreneurship. After reporting on a foundation that sponsored children’s education in war-torn Liberia, she felt the urgent need to give girls worldwide access to an education. This was a story she could not walk away from as an unbiased reporter, so she traveled to Liberia and began to volunteer for the cause after graduation.

Jancy

2013 Scholar from India

Meet Jancy, an 18-year-old college sophomore in India! After graduating from Shanti Bhavan, Jancy is attending Jyoti Nivas College where she is studying Econ-Tourism and singing in the choir! Jancy is also a fantastic artist and dreams of being a fashion designer – she was the judge of last year’s Tie-dye Cupcake Bake-off T-shirt design contest! She also is a master at henna. Jancy is the first girl in her family to have the opportunity to go to school and to learn to read and write.

Hillary Clinton

Philanthropist, Politician

Hillary Diane Rodham Clinton is a former United States Secretary of State, U.S. Senator, and First Lady of the United States. From 2009 to 2013, she was the 67th Secretary of State, serving under President Barack Obama. She previously represented New York in the U.S. Senate (2001 to 2009). Before that, as the wife of President Bill Clinton, she was First Lady from 1993 to 2001. In the 2008 election, Clinton was a leading candidate for the Democratic presidential nomination.
